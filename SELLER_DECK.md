## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: How accurate is the AI analysis?**
A: ContractShield uses Claude AI (Anthropic), one of the most advanced language models available. It identifies clause types, risk levels, and legal references with high accuracy. However, it's designed as a first-pass review tool — we always recommend lawyer review for high-stakes contracts.

**Q: Is my contract data stored?**
A: Uploaded files are processed in memory and deleted immediately after analysis. The analysis results are stored in your account for history, but the original document is not retained.

**Q: Can I self-host this?**
A: Yes. ContractShield uses SQLite (no external database), so you can deploy it on any Node.js server, VPS, or cloud provider. Full setup takes under 5 minutes.

**Q: What contract types are supported?**
A: Any contract in PDF or TXT format: NDAs, service agreements, employment contracts, leases, partnership agreements, subcontractor agreements, purchase orders, licensing agreements, consulting agreements, and more.

**Q: What languages are supported?**
A: 10 languages: English, Spanish, French, German, Portuguese, Chinese, Japanese, Korean, Arabic, Hindi. The entire analysis output is returned in your chosen language.

**Q: How is this different from ChatGPT?**
A: ChatGPT gives general responses. ContractShield gives structured, actionable analysis — risk scores, clause-by-clause breakdown, missing clause detection, compliance flags, and branded PDF reports. It's purpose-built for contract review with 49 specialized features.

**Q: Do I need a lawyer?**
A: For routine contracts (NDAs, freelance agreements, standard leases), ContractShield often provides enough insight to proceed confidently. For high-value or complex contracts, use ContractShield as a first pass, then share the report with your lawyer — it saves them time and saves you money.

**Q: What happens when I hit my plan limit?**
A: You'll see a prompt to upgrade. Your existing analyses and data are never deleted. You can upgrade or wait for the monthly reset.
